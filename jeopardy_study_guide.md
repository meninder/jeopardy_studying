## 10 High-Level Theme Areas

### 1. **American History & Culture**
The database heavily features American historical events, cultural institutions, and notable figures.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Civil War & Revolutionary War**: Key battles, figures like Lincoln, state secession order, War of 1812 treaties
- **20th Century Events**: Reagan era, Iran-Contra scandal, civil rights movement, Juneteenth, Draft riots
- **American Landmarks**: Statue of Liberty, Graceland, Portland Head Light, New York institutions (Penn Station)
- **Pop Culture Icons**: Elvis, baseball records, NFL teams, American authors (Melville, Hawthorne)
- **Constitutional & Legal History**: Preamble words, federal holidays, Supreme Court cases

### 2. **World Geography & International Relations**
Strong emphasis on physical geography, borders, and cultural geography worldwide.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Rivers & Bodies of Water**: Orinoco River (Colombia/Venezuela border), straits (Gibraltar, Hormuz, Tsushima)
- **Islands & Remote Locations**: Easter Island, Saint Helena, Falklands, Galapagos
- **European Geography**: Belgium (Ghent), Netherlands, Liechtenstein, Spain (Galicia), Italy (Venice)
- **Capital Cities**: Monrovia (Liberia), Dublin (Ireland), cities founded by freed slaves
- **Strategic Locations**: Persian Gulf oil routes, Antarctic regions, Polynesian boundaries

### 3. **Literature & Authors**
Extensive coverage of British, American, and world literature across centuries.

**Focus Areas:**
- **British Literature**: D.H. Lawrence, Dylan Thomas, Lord of the Flies, Great Expectations (Pip)
- **American Classics**: Moby Dick, Edgar Allan Poe ("Tell-Tale Heart"), The Outsiders
- **20th Century Novels**: F. Scott Fitzgerald, Vonnegut, Shirley Jackson, John Grisham
- **Scary/Horror Books**: Psycho (Robert Bloch), Let the Right One In, Helter Skelter
- **Book Dedications & Inspirations**: Melville to Hawthorne, medical dramas based on real doctors

### 4. **Entertainment: Film, TV & Music**
Heavy representation of movies, television shows, and popular music across decades.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Classic Films**: Citizen Kane, Die Hard (Nakatomi Plaza), Pretty Woman, Muppet Treasure Island
- **Recent Productions**: Belfast (Branagh), Beetlejuice Beetlejuice, Severance, The Bear
- **TV History**: A Different World (Hillman College), Brooklyn Nine-Nine, Fraggle Rock, The Knick
- **Music Hits**: "Great Balls of Fire" (Jerry Lee Lewis), "MMMBop" (Hanson), "Party in the U.S.A."
- **Broadway**: Annie Get Your Gun ("Anything You Can Do"), jukebox musicals

### 5. **Science & Medicine**
Brain anatomy, medical conditions, and scientific principles appear frequently.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Brain Anatomy**: Cerebellum ("little brain"), medulla oblongata (respiration), synapses, concussions
- **Medical Conditions**: Encephalitis, pemphigus vulgaris, dermatitis, diaper rash, tuberculosis (TB)
- **Chemistry**: Mercury (Hg), tritium (hydrogen isotope), chemical elements and symbols
- **Biology**: Metabolism (sloths have lowest), castoreum from beavers, penguin species
- **Physics & Engineering**: Pumps (sump, slurry), siphons, tectonic activity

### 6. **Arts & Artists**
Visual arts, museums, and artistic movements feature prominently.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Old Masters**: Rembrandt, Vermeer ("The Milkmaid"), Italian Renaissance painters
- **Museums & Collections**: Guggenheim (Peggy, Venice), Louvre (I.M. Pei's pyramid), art acquisitions
- **Tapestries & Historical Art**: Bayeux Tapestry (230 feet, Bishop Odo), wall hangings
- **20th Century Artists**: Magritte ("Empire of Light"), Kandinsky, Koons (wine labels)
- **Art Patronage**: Rothschild estate (Château Mouton), artists on wine labels

### 7. **Food, Drink & Culinary Culture**
Beverages, baked goods, and international cuisine are well-represented.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Cocktails & Drinks**: B-52 shooter (Kahlúa, Baileys, Grand Marnier), Bellini (peach & Prosecco), sake
- **Wine Knowledge**: White wine at galleries, Prosecco vs champagne, Château Mouton Rothschild
- **Baked Goods**: Jelly roll (spiral sponge cake), German chocolate cake, macaroons, crumpets
- **Regional Specialties**: Chicago's Mr. Beef (The Bear inspiration), voodoo donuts, Filipino cuisine
- **International Foods**: Lederhosen at Oktoberfest, British breakfast, Thai pad see ew

### 8. **Language, Wordplay & Vocabulary**
Significant focus on etymology, homophones, rhymes, and word construction.

**Focus Areas:**
- **Word Origins**: Latin roots (mortician from death), Spanish borrowings (sombrero), French terms (trousseau)
- **Homophones**: Swayed/suede, peek/peer, pip (fruit seed/dice dot)
- **Multi-syllable Words**: Prerequisites (4 syllables), pitchfork (9 letters), haberdasher (11 letters)
- **Compound Words**: Redcoats, word formations, doubled consonants
- **Abbreviations**: "Prep" school (preparatory), "avails" (availability), CB radio (Citizens Band)

### 9. **Royalty, Historical Figures & Biography**
Kings, queens, and notable historical personalities across cultures.

**Focus Areas:**
- **European Royalty**: Louis XIV (Sun King at age 4), Prince Andrew (Falklands), Queen Christina (Sweden)
- **Abdications**: Netherlands queens (1948, 1980), succession patterns
- **Political Leaders**: Desmond Tutu (Nobel 1984), Gorbachev (Chernobyl), Mandela & Winnie
- **American First Ladies**: Mary Todd Lincoln (sanitarium, died Springfield 1882)
- **Religious Leaders**: Chief rabbi, chief imam, Baptist preachers in NFL

### 10. **Sports & Athletics**
Football, baseball, and various sports references with specific records and facts.

**Focus Areas:**
- **NFL Records**: Wayne Gretzky hat tricks (50), defensive legends (Reggie White), team histories
- **Team Locations**: Cleveland Browns (Dawg Pound), New York Giants (ticker-tape parades)
- **Stadium Foods**: Lucas Oil Stadium (Indianapolis Colts), loaded fries variations
- **Baseball History**: Records, Negro Leagues, team mascots (Maryland Terrapins)
- **Athletic Achievements**: Amelia Earhart (1932 transatlantic flight), Olympic ceremonies (Sydney 2000)

## Study Strategy Recommendations

1. **For Geography**: Focus on borders, rivers as boundaries, strategic waterways, and island nations
2. **For Literature**: Know major works, their first lines, character names, and dedication contexts
3. **For History**: Memorize key dates, scandal names (Iran-Contra), and sequential events
4. **For Science**: Brain parts and their functions are heavily tested
5. **For Wordplay**: Practice homophones, Latin/Greek roots, and abbreviation expansions
6. **For Pop Culture**: Know recent (2020s) and classic (1980s) entertainment equally
7. **For Food & Drink**: Cocktail ingredients and regional specialties appear frequently
8. **For Arts**: Museum locations and artist-artwork pairings are common
9. **For Royalty**: Focus on succession, abdication, and 20th century figures
10. **For Sports**: Team nicknames, records, and cross-references (also NBA team names)
